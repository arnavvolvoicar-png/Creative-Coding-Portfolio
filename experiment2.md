# Experiment 2

In the base version of Experiment 2, I created a simple city landscape generator that produced a new skyline every time the sketch refreshed. The buildings were generated using random heights, widths, and positions, which gave each run a unique silhouette. This stage helped me understand how procedural generation works when combining randomness with repeated shapes. I also experimented with basic window patterns and simple sky colours to give the scene more atmosphere.

[Basic City Landscape](/2.1/index.html)

In the second version, I expanded the city generator to include more building types and visual variation. I added additional shapes, improved the window layouts by increasing the number of columns, and introduced more structured patterns so the buildings felt less repetitive. I also upgraded the sky by experimenting with gradients and a night‑mode version, which made the scene feel more atmospheric and alive. These changes made the generator feel more intentional and visually rich while still keeping the procedural randomness that makes each run unique.

[Improved Version for City Landscape](/2.2/index.html)

For the final version, I shifted away from the city generator and developed a two‑player game inspired by “Puck It.” I started with a basic version where two players could move paddles and hit a puck across the screen. After getting the core mechanics working, I improved the physics so the puck moved more smoothly and reacted more realistically to collisions. I also added colour changes and visual feedback to make the game feel more polished and responsive. This version was a big change from the earlier city generator, but it allowed me to explore interaction and movement.
